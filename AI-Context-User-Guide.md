# AI Context User Guide

This guide explains what information the AI assistant has access to when helping you with your scorecard, so you can better understand how to work with it effectively.

## Overview

The AI assistant in your scorecard application has access to different types of information depending on how you're using it. Understanding this helps you ask better questions and get more relevant responses.

## When the AI Has Access to What Information

### 1. AI Chat with Items Selected

**When this happens**: You select specific pillars, categories, goals, or programs before opening the AI chat.

**What the AI knows**:
- Only the items you've selected and their details
- Any special AI instructions written for those programs
- How your selected items connect to other parts of the scorecard (alignments)
- The complete structure showing how everything relates to each other

**Best for**: Getting focused help on specific areas, analyzing particular programs, or understanding connections between selected items.

### 2. AI Chat with Everything

**When this happens**: You open the AI chat without selecting any specific items.

**What the AI knows**:
- Your entire scorecard - all pillars, categories, goals, and programs
- All quarterly objectives and status updates
- All progress updates and comments
- Special AI instructions for all programs

**Best for**: High-level analysis, comparing across your entire scorecard, strategic planning, or when you're not sure which specific items to focus on.

### 3. Generate Progress Updates

**When this happens**: You click "Generate Update" on a program's progress section.

**What the AI knows**:
- Everything about that specific program (objectives, status, personnel, existing updates)
- Any special instructions written for that program
- How that program connects to other items (if applicable)
- Any documents you upload to provide additional context
- Your specific instructions for what you want the update to include

**Best for**: Creating detailed progress reports, incorporating new information, or updating stakeholders on specific programs.

### 4. AI Flows (Special Analysis Tools)

**When this happens**: You use tools like "Goal Comparison" or "Learnings & Best Practices" from the main page.

**What the AI knows**:
- The items you've selected for analysis
- How those items connect to other parts of your scorecard
- Any documents you upload for additional context
- The specific type of analysis you're requesting

**Best for**: Specialized analysis tasks, comparing different approaches, extracting insights, or generating reports for specific purposes.

## Special AI Instructions

### What They Are
Some programs have special "AI Context" fields where you can write specific instructions or background information just for the AI. Think of these as "notes to the AI assistant" about how to handle that particular program.

### When to Use Them
- Your program has unique circumstances the AI should know about
- There are specific constraints or considerations for that program
- You want the AI to focus on particular aspects when discussing the program
- There's background context that helps the AI give better advice

### Examples of Good AI Instructions
- "This program depends on external vendor delivery - focus on risk mitigation"
- "Budget constraints are the primary challenge - prioritize cost-effective solutions"
- "This is a pilot program - emphasize learning and adaptation over strict timelines"

## Tips for Working with the AI

### Getting Better Responses
1. **Be specific about what you want** - Instead of "help with this program," try "help me identify risks in Q3 objectives"
2. **Select relevant items** - Choose the specific pillars/programs you want to discuss for more focused help
3. **Use the right tool** - Progress updates for detailed reporting, chat for analysis and questions
4. **Provide context** - Upload relevant documents or mention recent changes that might affect your situation

### Understanding AI Limitations
- The AI only knows what's in your scorecard data and what you tell it
- It doesn't have access to external systems, emails, or conversations
- It can't see real-time changes unless you refresh the data
- It works best when you provide clear, specific requests

## Data Privacy and Security

The AI assistant only has access to:
- Information in your scorecard database
- Documents you explicitly upload during your session
- Instructions you provide in your messages

It does not have access to:
- Other applications or systems
- Your email or communication tools
- Personal files outside of what you share
- Information from other users or organizations

## Getting the Most Value

### For Strategic Planning
Use the full scorecard view to analyze patterns, identify gaps, and get recommendations across your entire organization.

### For Program Management
Select specific programs and use progress update generation to create detailed status reports and identify next steps.

### For Analysis and Insights
Use AI Flows with selected items to compare approaches, extract lessons learned, and generate insights for stakeholders.

### For Problem Solving
Select the relevant items and provide specific context about challenges you're facing to get targeted recommendations and solutions.
